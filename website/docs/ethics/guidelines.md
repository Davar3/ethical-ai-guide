# Ethical Guidelines

Using AI responsibly in academic research.

## OECD AI Principles

The internationally recognized framework for ethical AI:

1. **Transparency** - Be clear about AI use
2. **Accountability** - You remain responsible
3. **Fairness** - Avoid biased outputs
4. **Privacy** - Protect data
5. **Human Control** - AI assists, doesn't replace you

---

## Academic Integrity Rules

### ✅ Acceptable Uses

- Brainstorming and ideation
- Grammar and style checking
- Code assistance and debugging
- Learning concepts
- Summarizing your own uploaded papers

### ❌ Unacceptable Uses

- Submitting AI-written text as your own
- Using AI to complete assignments without disclosure
- Fabricating data or citations
- Bypassing plagiarism detection

---

## Best Practices

### 1. Disclose AI Use

Be transparent about how you used AI in your research process.

!!! example "Sample Disclosure"
    *"ChatGPT (GPT-4) was used for brainstorming research questions and improving grammar. All content was written by the author and verified independently."*

### 2. Maintain Authorship

- **You** are the author
- **You** are responsible for accuracy
- **You** must verify all information

### 3. Critical Review

Never accept AI output without:

- Reading it carefully
- Verifying claims
- Rewriting in your own voice
- Checking for factual errors

---

## The Bottom Line

!!! success "Golden Rule"
    **AI is a tool, not a co-author.** Use it to enhance your work, but ensure everything you submit is your own verified work that you fully understand.
